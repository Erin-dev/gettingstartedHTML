Sheryl Lee Ralph from "Moesha"

Sheryl Lee Ralph was born December 30, 1956 in Waterbury, Connecticut, USA. She is known for her roles in Moesha (1996), Its A Living (1980), and Instant Mom (2013). Her Career Began in the late 1970s. She has been Married to Vincent Hughes since July 30, 2005 a Pennsylvania state senator. Her first leading role in a film came as Denzel Washington's wife in The Mighty Quinn, released in 1989.

Factoids

At 19, Ralph was the youngest woman to ever graduate from Rutgers University; during her time at Rutgers, Ralph was one of the earliest winners of the Irene Ryan Acting Scholarships. Also, she appeared in the music video and sang in the choir on the song "Voices That Care." She once studied medicine at Rutgers University. She was named Best College Actress in 1974. In 1973, she was recognized as Miss Black Teen-Age New York

Latest Works

On June 16, 2009, it was announced that Ralph would join the cast of the Broadway-bound musical The First Wives Club as Elyse. In 2011, Ralph guest-starred in the Young Justice episode "Terrors" as Amanda Waller. In 2013, Ralph appeared in the NBC television show Smash as Cynthia, the mother of Jennifer Hudson's character.
